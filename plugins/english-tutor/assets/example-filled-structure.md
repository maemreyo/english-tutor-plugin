> [!info] Structural Analysis
> **Structure Type:** Pattern
> 
> **Formula / Pattern:**
> ```text
> The + [Comparative Adj/Adv] + [S1] + [V1], the + [Comparative Adj/Adv] + [S2] + [V2]
> ```
> 
> **Components:**
> - **The**: Definite article used as an adverb here.
> - **Comparative**: Adjective or adverb in comparative form (e.g., more, better, faster, harder).
> - **S + V**: Subject and Verb clauses.
>
> **Formation Rules:**
> 1. Use comma to separate the two clauses.
> 2. If "be" is the verb, it can sometimes be omitted in informal contexts (e.g., "The sooner, the better").

> [!abstract] Meaning & Usage
> **Core Meaning:**
> - **Literal:** As one thing increases/changes, the other thing increases/changes in response.
> - **Figurative:** Cause and effect relationship between two varying quantities.
> 
> **Functional Meaning:**
> - **Purpose:** To express correlation or proportional change.
> - **Nuance:** Can be used for advice, warnings, or general observations.
> 
> **English Definition:**
> - Used to say that two things change together; as one amount or degree increases, another also increases (or decreases).

## ðŸ§  Spaced Repetition Flashcards

### Card 1: Pattern Recognition
**Question::** What is the structure pattern for **The more... the more...**?
**Answer::** The + [Comparative] + S + V, the + [Comparative] + S + V

### Card 2: Meaning
**Question::** What does **The more... the more...** express?
**Answer::** Describes how two things vary together (correlation).

### Card 3: Usage
**Question::** When should you use **The more... the more...**?
**Answer::** To show cause and effect where the degree of one thing depends on the degree of another.

### Card 4: Example
**Question::** Create a sentence with **The more... the more...** about [learning]
**Answer::** The more you practice English, the better you become.

### Card 5: Error Correction
**Question::** What's wrong with: "More you give, more you get."
**Answer::** Missing "The". Correct: "**The** more you give, **the** more you get."

### Card 6: Comparison
**Question::** How is **The more... the more...** different from **If... then...**?
**Answer::** "The more..." implies a continuous change/degree, while "If... then..." is often a binary condition.

### Card 7: Transformation
**Question::** Transform this sentence using **The more... the more...**: "If you work hard, you will be successful."
**Answer::** The harder you work, the more successful you will be.
